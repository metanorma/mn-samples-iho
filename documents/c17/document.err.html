<title>What is a Spatial Data Infrastructure (SDI)?</title>
<p id="_9784cdd8-d26b-d18c-51e8-2e94c2296a82">Spatial Data is the data or information that identifies the geographic location of features and boundaries on Earth and Space, such as natural or constructed features, oceans and space but also includes encoding attributes, observations and other metrics concerning these features and boundaries.</p>

<p id="_9b32c646-6d13-14f0-d4b7-0b88ce5c4863">Spatial data is usually stored as coordinates and topology, and is data that can be mapped. Spatial data is often accessed, manipulated or analyzed through Geographic Information Systems (GIS).</p>

<clause id="_what_role_should_a_ho_have_in_msdi" obligation="normative">
<title>What role should a HO have in MSDI?</title>
<p id="_8ce39f50-a0e7-36bd-feba-6faee89355d2">Most HOs hold data in order to support nautical charting requirements with limited emphasis placed on providing that same data to support wider environmental and commercial coastal and offshore activities.</p>

<p id="_2ec9d992-63c1-c708-83e7-12c4a078d8c5">MSDI places a greater emphasis on the unlocking of all geospatial information, including hydrographic information, and to make that information more widely available to support the myriad of uses described in <xref target="annexB"/> of this document. HOs are well placed to support SDIs in that it provides a natural extension in the management and dissemination of the underpinning hydrographic information to a wider user community in an integrated manner. All HOs should therefore carefully consider how they might engage and play a full role in the development of, or participation in a SDI. <xref target="annexG"/> show possible ways for a HO to engage in SDI.</p>
